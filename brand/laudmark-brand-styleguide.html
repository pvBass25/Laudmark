TrustWall brand system

Three fresh, honest directions for a testimonial tool whose entire promise is trust — built to look like nothing else in the category. Every color here is verified to WCAG AA, and in each system one interactive color does all the work: links and buttons are the same.

Trustworthy Honest Fresh — not category-default WCAG AA verified Drop-in Tailwind tokens
01 · Positioning

Why these look different on purpose

TrustWall sells trust: real consent, no fabrication, SEO-honest embeds. The brand has to feel credible at a glance — but the review/testimonial category is a monoculture of two colors. Standing out is itself a trust signal: it reads as a real, considered product rather than another clone.

The category we’re escaping

Blue/indigo, violet/purple and review-green are the reflexive defaults. All three directions below deliberately avoid them.

Where we’re playing instead

2026 brand research points to the open lane: warm earth tones (clay, terracotta, sandstone) that read as “trust, calmness, long-term value,” deep-ink contrast with a single confident accent, smoky jewel tones, and a serif revival that signals authority + warmth.

04 · How to apply

One block to reskin the app

All three directions are pre-written in brand/trustwall-design-tokens.css. To apply one, copy its @theme block into app/globals.css, replacing the current “Indigo Trust” block, and add the matching font link. No component markup changes — every bg-brand / text-ink utility already points at these tokens.

/* app/globals.css — Direction A · Testament */
@theme {
  --color-canvas:     #FBF8F2;
  --color-ink:        #211C16;
  --color-brand:      #A8531B;  /* links + buttons */
  --color-brand-strong: #844014;
  /* …full block in trustwall-design-tokens.css */
}
